Orange Chicken & BBQ Sauce

BBQ Summer Chicken

Taking advantage of the warm weather this weekend, I decided to take the barbecue at my parents out for it's first spin of the year.

Ingredients

  • For the Orange Chicken
  • 1 Medium Orange
  • 1 Whole Chicken
  • 2 Garlic Cloves (Diced)
  • 1 Tablespoon of Cider Vinegar
  • 2 Tablespoons of Honey
  • 1 Teaspoon of Paprika
  • 1 Teaspoon of Dried Rosemary
  • 2 Pinches of Sea Salt
  • For the Barbecue Sauce
  • 200ml of Ketchup
  • 100ml of Honey
  • 50g of Sugar
  • 50ml of Cider Vinegar
  • 1 Tablespoon of Wholegrain Mustard
  • 30ml of Bourbon (optional)

Method

This recipe should take 3-4 hours to complete

    For the Orange Chicken

  1. Take the chicken and remove the backbone (if you don't want to do this your butcher will do it for you)
  2. Juice the orange into a glass and throw in the honey, diced garlic, cider vinegar, rosemary, paprika and mix well.
  3. Next place the chicken in a deep oven proof tray and cut the skin and breast (around 1cm deep) with a knife.
  4. Pour the marinade over the chicken, wrap the tray in clingfilm and leave to rest in your fridge for 2 hours (or longer if you can).
  5. When thoroughly rested remove the chicken from the fridge and allow to rise to room temperature (should take twenty/thirty minutes).
  6. Turn your oven on to 200oC and put the chicken in on the middle shelf for 1-1.5 hours (cook for 10 minutes less than the cooking instructions state). Remembering to regularly take your chicken out to baste in the marinade (I do mine roughly every 15 minutes)
  7. Head out to your barbecue and fire it up to a medium heat
  8. When the chicken is done, take it out of the oven and bring it straight over to the grill top and place it onto the grill with the open side facing down.

    For the Barbecue Sauce

  1. Whilst the chicken is on the barbecue add all the ingredients of the barbecue sauce to a pan and place on a medium heat. My parents barbecue has a side burner, which is super handy in times like this.
  2. Combine the ingredients in the pan using a spatula and keep stirring as the liquid begins to reduce down.
  3. After around 5 minutes taste the mixture and if it's too sweet add more vinegar, too sour add more honey/sugar.
  4. When you're happy with the taste, remove from the heat and scoop into a container to serve.
