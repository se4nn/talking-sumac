Crispy Chicken & Hot Honey Sauce

Chicken Treat

I've always been a fan of fried chicken, but as I try to eat healthier and leaner meals these days I've struggled to get rid of that craving. Last night I thought I would have a go at cooking up a reasonably healthy alternative.

Ingredients

  • For the chicken
  • 750g of Chicken Breast
  • 2 Eggs
  • 200g of Oats
  • 100g of Breadcrumbs
  • 2 Tablespoons of Hot Paprika
  • 1 Teaspoon of Sea Salt
  • Smattering of Cracked Black Pepper
  • Drizzle of Olive Oil
  • For the Hot Honey Sauce
  • 100g of Honey
  • 15ml of Balsamic Vinegar
  • 2 Tablespoons of Paprika
  • 1 Tablespoon of Wholegrain Mustard

Method

This recipe should take around 40 minutes to complete

    For the Crispy Chicken

  1. Set your oven to 230oC.
  2. Slice the chicken breast up into tender sized pieces
  3. Mix the oats, breadcrumbs, paprika, salt and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Crack both eggs into a separate bowl and combine.
  5. Take the chicken pieces one by one and dip them first in the egg then into the breadcrumb topping before putting them onto a grill tray.
  6. When all your chicken pieces are done grab your olive oil and drizzle over the top.
  7. Place the tray into the oven for around 30 minutes turning once whilst cooking (don't worry if you lose some of your crumb topping here).

    For the Hot Honey Sauce

  1. Place a non-stick pan onto a medium heat.
  2. Add your honey into the pan and wait for it to begin to bubble.
  3. When the honey is bubbling add the balsamic vinegar, paprika and wholegrain mustard to it and stir to combine.
  4. Keep on a low heat until your chicken is ready to serve, the place into a small bowl for everyone to dig into.
